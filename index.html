Baiting Systems

Baiting systems are highly effective for controlling pests like ants and roaches. These systems use attractive food sources laced with slow-acting insecticides. Pests consume the bait and carry it back to their colonies, spreading the poison and eliminating the entire population. This method is particularly effective because it targets pests at their source and minimizes pesticide exposure.

Trapping

Trapping is an excellent option for controlling rodents and other larger pests. We use a variety of traps, including snap traps, glue boards, and live traps, to capture pests without using harmful chemicals. Seeing Pests Regularly

Unexplained Property Damage

Gnaw marks on furniture, holes in walls, or damaged wiring could indicate the presence of rodents or termites. Scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds coming from your walls or attic at night are often signs of rodent activity. Rodents are nocturnal creatures, and their presence can be disruptive and unsettling.
